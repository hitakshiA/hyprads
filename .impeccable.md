## Design Context

### Users
**Primary**: Neeraj Joshi (Troopod founder, BITS Pilani grad, second-time founder) evaluating this as an AI PM take-home assignment. He's seen hundreds of pitches and demos — he'll spot generic AI slop instantly. He cares about: does it work, does it show product thinking, does it show CRO understanding.

**Secondary (implied by the product)**: Performance marketers at Indian D2C brands (Mokobara, Perfora, Bombay Shaving Company type). They manage 80-150 active ad campaigns across Meta/Google. They think in conversion rates, ROAS, and bounce rates. They're busy, impatient, and skeptical of AI tools that overpromise.

**The job to be done**: "I ran an ad campaign. I need the landing page to match the ad so people don't bounce. Show me it works in under 5 minutes."

### Brand Personality
**Sharp. Confident. Results-driven.**

This is a CRO tool, not a design tool. The interface should feel like a command center for conversions — like the person using it knows exactly what they're doing. No hand-holding, no fluff, no "powered by AI" badges. The AI is invisible; the results speak.

Emotional goals: competence, trust, "this person gets it"

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Dark mode primary** — performance marketing tools live in dark UIs (Meta Ads Manager, analytics dashboards, ad platforms). Dark signals professional tooling, not consumer SaaS.
- **Accent color**: A sharp, warm accent — not Troopod's blue, not generic purple. Think coral/orange or electric green against dark. Something that says "conversion" and "action."
- **Typography**: One sharp sans-serif. Not Inter (overused). Something with character that still reads as technical. Geist, Satoshi, or similar.
- **Data-forward**: Numbers, metrics, and results should be visually prominent. The CRO mindset is quantitative.
- **Before/after is the hero moment**: The UI should make the comparison between original and personalized page dramatic and obvious.

### Anti-references
- Generic SaaS landing page templates (gradient hero, card grid, "trusted by" logos)
- Anything that looks like it was made with shadcn defaults and no thought
- "AI tool" aesthetic: purple gradients, sparkle emojis, "magic" language
- Toy demos that feel like a hackathon project
- Over-designed interfaces that prioritize looking cool over showing results

### References
- Linear app (sharp, dark, purposeful)
- Vercel dashboard (clean data presentation)
- Meta Ads Manager (the tool their users already live in)
- Troopod's own site feel — professional but not over-polished

### Design Principles

1. **Show the work, not the magic.** The streaming stages exist for a reason — they show the user exactly what the AI is doing. This builds trust and demonstrates competence. No vague loading spinners.

2. **Results first, explanation second.** The before/after comparison is the money shot. Everything else (ad analysis, change report) is supporting evidence. Lead with the result.

3. **Respect the evaluator's intelligence.** Neeraj has seen hundreds of demos. Don't over-explain. Don't add tooltips to obvious things. Don't use marketing language. Let the product demonstrate its own value.

4. **One page, one flow.** Input → Progress → Result. No navigation, no settings page, no dashboard. This is a demo of one capability done extremely well. The 1:1 attention ratio principle from CRO applies to this UI too.

5. **The personalized page IS the proof.** The iframe showing the personalized page must be prominent, full-width, and impressive. If the person looking at it thinks "wait, this actually looks like a real landing page," you've won.

- Keyboard navigable
